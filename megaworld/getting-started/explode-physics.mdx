**Explode Physics** applies a radial outward impulse to existing objects in your scene, scattering them using physics simulation. This creates a natural, chaotic distribution that is difficult to achieve with procedural scatter alone — useful for rubble fields, fallen debris, scattered items, or any layout where objects should appear randomly thrown or scattered by an impact.

---

## Step 1 — Open the MegaWorld Window and select Explode Physics

Press **Alt + Z** to open the MegaWorld Window. In the **Tools Panel**, click the **+** button and choose **Explode Physics**.

---

## Step 2 — Select which prototypes to explode

In the **Selection Panel**, choose the Types and prototypes you want to affect.

<Tip>
  Prefabs need Collider and Rigidbody components (or MegaWorld will add temporary ones) for the impulse to work correctly.
</Tip>

---

## Step 3 — Configure the explosion

In the **Settings Panel**, configure:

- **Explosion Radius** — how far the impulse reaches.
- **Explosion Force** — the strength of the outward push.
- Additional physics parameters that control how objects come to rest after the explosion.

---

## Step 4 — Trigger the explosion in the scene view

Click in the **Scene View** at the explosion origin point. All selected prototype objects within the radius receive the impulse and scatter outward, then settle under gravity.

---

## What's next

- Use [Brush Physics](/megaworld/getting-started/brush-physics) to settle the scattered objects smoothly onto the terrain.
- Use [Brush Erase](/megaworld/getting-started/brush-erase) to remove stray objects that landed in unwanted places.
- Read the full [Explode Physics](/megaworld/tools/explode-physics) reference documentation.
