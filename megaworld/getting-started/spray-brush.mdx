The **Spray Brush** is an Editor Window tool for quickly painting large numbers of objects in a spray pattern. It is designed for fast coverage rather than precise control — ideal for filling areas with grass clumps, ground cover, or scattered debris where volume matters more than placement exactness.

---

## Step 1 — Open the MegaWorld Window and select Spray Brush

Press **Alt + Z** to open the MegaWorld Window. In the **Tools Panel**, click the **+** button and choose **Spray Brush**.

---

## Step 2 — Add a Type and prototypes

In the **Selection Panel**, click **Add Type**. Inside the Type, click **Add Resource** and choose **Unity/GameObject** or **Unity/Terrain Detail**. Drag your assets into the prototype list.

---

## Step 3 — Set brush size and spray density

In the **Settings Panel**, adjust:

- **Brush Size** — how wide the spray covers.
- **Spawn Count** — how many objects are placed per spray interval.

---

## Step 4 — Spray in the scene view

Move your cursor into the **Scene View** and hold the mouse button. Objects continuously spray into the brush area as long as you hold.

---

## What's next

- Use [Brush Erase](/megaworld/getting-started/brush-erase) to remove overpainted areas.
- Add [Mask Filters](/megaworld/mask-filters/overview) to prevent spraying on steep slopes.
- Read the full [Spray Brush](/megaworld/tools/spray-brush) reference documentation.
