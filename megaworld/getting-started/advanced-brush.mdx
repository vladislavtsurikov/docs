The **Advanced Brush** lets you paint objects directly in the scene view. Unlike the Terrain Spawner which fills an area in one pass, the Advanced Brush lets you direct exactly where objects appear by clicking and dragging across the scene. It supports all four MegaWorld resource types and runs the same filter, scatter, transform, and overlap-check systems as the procedural tools.

---

## Step 1 — Open the MegaWorld Window and select Advanced Brush

Press **Alt + Z** to open the MegaWorld Window. In the **Tools Panel**, click the **+** button and choose **Advanced Brush**, or click an existing Advanced Brush instance.

---

## Step 2 — Add a Group and prototypes

In the **Selection Panel**, click **Add Type**. Inside the Group, click **Add Resource** and choose **Unity/GameObject**. Drag your Prefabs (trees, rocks, props) into the prototype list.

<Note>
  Add several Prefabs to the same Group. Advanced Brush randomly picks among them while you paint, which naturally breaks up visual repetition.
</Note>

---

## Step 3 — Configure brush settings

In the **Settings Panel**, adjust:

- **Brush Size** — controls the radius of the painted area.
- **Spacing** — controls how far the cursor must move before another spawn point fires during a drag stroke.
- **Scatter Settings** — choose how spawn points are distributed inside the brush area (random, grid, Poisson disc, and others).

Optionally add [Mask Filters](/megaworld/mask-filters/overview) to restrict painting to specific heights, slopes, or terrain textures.

Add [Transform Components](/megaworld/transform-components/overview) for rotation and scale variation on each placed object.

---

## Step 4 — Paint in the scene view

Move your cursor into the **Scene View**. A circular brush preview shows the area that will be affected.

- **Click** to paint once at the cursor position.
- **Click and drag** to paint continuously across the scene.

---

## What's next

- Enable **Spherical** mode in [Brush Settings](/megaworld/shared-settings/brush-settings) to paint on vertical or curved surfaces.
- Use [Brush Modify](/megaworld/getting-started/brush-modify) to adjust already-painted objects without removing them.
- Use [Brush Erase](/megaworld/getting-started/brush-erase) to remove painted objects.
- Read the full [Advanced Brush](/megaworld/tools/advanced-brush) reference documentation.
