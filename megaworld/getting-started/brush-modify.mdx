The **Brush Modify** tool adjusts the transform properties of objects that are already in your scene. Instead of adding or removing objects, it applies position, rotation, and scale changes as you drag the brush across them. Use it to break up repetition in a procedural pass or to add hand-crafted variation to existing placement.

---

## Step 1 — Open the MegaWorld Window and select Brush Modify

Press **Alt + Z** to open the MegaWorld Window. In the **Tools Panel**, click the **+** button and choose **Brush Modify**.

---

## Step 2 — Select which prototypes to modify

In the **Selection Panel**, choose the Types and prototypes you want the brush to affect. Objects not in the selection are ignored by the brush.

---

## Step 3 — Configure the Modify Transform Stack

In the **Settings Panel**, enable the transform components you want to apply:

- **Position** — shift objects up, down, or laterally.
- **Rotation** — randomly rotate objects around one or more axes.
- **Scale** — increase or decrease object scale.
- **Randomize Scale** — add random scale variation on each stroke.

---

## Step 4 — Optionally add filters

Add [Mask Filters](/megaworld/mask-filters/overview) to restrict which objects get modified. For example, use a **Slope Filter** to only modify objects on flat ground and leave cliff-placed objects untouched.

---

## Step 5 — Paint in the scene view

Move your cursor into the **Scene View** and drag the brush over existing objects. The modify stack is applied to all objects within the brush radius that pass the filter.

<Tip>
  Brush Modify works best after a large procedural spawn pass. Generate the base layer with Terrain Spawner first, then use Brush Modify to add hand-directed variation.
</Tip>

---

## What's next

- Use [Edit](/megaworld/getting-started/edit) for precise single-object adjustments.
- Use [Brush Erase](/megaworld/getting-started/brush-erase) to remove objects from over-populated areas.
- Read the full [Brush Modify](/megaworld/tools/brush-modify) reference documentation.
