**Precise Physics** is an Editor Window tool that runs a targeted physics simulation on individual or small groups of objects. Where [Brush Physics](/megaworld/getting-started/brush-physics) affects everything in a brush radius, Precise Physics gives you finer control over which objects are simulated and how the simulation runs — useful for settling specific props that look out of place without disturbing the rest of the scene.

---

## Step 1 — Open the MegaWorld Window and select Precise Physics

Press **Alt + Z** to open the MegaWorld Window. In the **Tools Panel**, click the **+** button and choose **Precise Physics**.

---

## Step 2 — Select which prototypes to simulate

In the **Selection Panel**, choose the Types and prototypes you want to affect.

---

## Step 3 — Configure simulation settings

In the **Settings Panel**, adjust the physics parameters — simulation duration, gravity scale, and any damping settings that control how objects come to rest.

---

## Step 4 — Interact in the scene view

Click on objects in the **Scene View** to select them for simulation. Run the simulation and the selected objects settle onto the terrain and surrounding colliders.

---

## What's next

- Use [Brush Physics](/megaworld/getting-started/brush-physics) for broad-area settling.
- Use [Explode Physics](/megaworld/getting-started/explode-physics) to scatter objects with an impulse force.
- Read the full [Precise Physics](/megaworld/tools/precise-physics) reference documentation.
