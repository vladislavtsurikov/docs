The **Brush Erase** tool removes already-placed objects from the scene using a circular brush. It is the dedicated cleanup tool for any MegaWorld placement pass — use it to clear specific areas, cut paths through vegetation, or remove unwanted objects that survived a procedural spawn.

---

## Step 1 — Open the MegaWorld Window and select Brush Erase

Press **Alt + Z** to open the MegaWorld Window. In the **Tools Panel**, click the **+** button and choose **Brush Erase**.

---

## Step 2 — Select which prototypes to erase

In the **Selection Panel**, choose the Types and prototypes you want to erase. Only the selected prototypes will be removed — others are left untouched.

<Tip>
  Disable a prototype in the Selection Panel to temporarily protect it from erasure without removing it from the list.
</Tip>

---

## Step 3 — Set brush size and strength

In the **Settings Panel**, adjust the **Brush Size** to control how wide the erase area is. A larger brush clears more at once; a smaller brush is better for detailed cleanup.

---

## Step 4 — Erase in the scene view

Move your cursor into the **Scene View** and click or drag to erase all selected prototypes within the brush radius.

---

## What's next

- Use [Mask Filters](/megaworld/mask-filters/overview) to erase only within a specific height or slope range.
- Use [Brush Modify](/megaworld/getting-started/brush-modify) if you want to adjust objects rather than delete them.
- Use [Edit](/megaworld/getting-started/edit) to remove individual objects one by one.
- Read the full [Brush Erase](/megaworld/tools/brush-erase) reference documentation.
