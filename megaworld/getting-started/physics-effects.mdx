**Physics Effects** is an Editor Window tool that applies continuous or configurable physics forces to objects that are already placed in your scene. Unlike the settle-based tools (Brush Physics, Precise Physics), Physics Effects is used to push, slide, or move objects using forces — useful for interactive environment dressing or controlled repositioning using physics.

---

## Step 1 — Open the MegaWorld Window and select Physics Effects

Press **Alt + Z** to open the MegaWorld Window. In the **Tools Panel**, click the **+** button and choose **Physics Effects**.

---

## Step 2 — Select which prototypes to affect

In the **Selection Panel**, choose the Types and prototypes you want to apply physics effects to.

---

## Step 3 — Configure the effect

In the **Settings Panel**, set the force type, direction, and magnitude of the physics effect.

---

## Step 4 — Apply in the scene view

Click or drag in the **Scene View** to apply the physics effect to objects within the active area.

---

## What's next

- Use [Explode Physics](/megaworld/getting-started/explode-physics) for a one-shot radial scatter.
- Use [Brush Physics](/megaworld/getting-started/brush-physics) to settle objects after applying effects.
- Read the full [Physics Effects](/megaworld/tools/physics-effects) reference documentation.
