**Precise Place** is an Editor Window tool for placing individual objects one at a time with exact control. Unlike brush tools that scatter many objects at once, Precise Place lets you click a specific point in the scene and place a single prototype there — with optional snapping, raycast alignment, and transform offsets. Use it for hero props, focal objects, or any placement where position matters precisely.

---

## Step 1 — Open the MegaWorld Window and select Precise Place

Press **Alt + Z** to open the MegaWorld Window. In the **Tools Panel**, click the **+** button and choose **Precise Place**.

---

## Step 2 — Add a Type and prototype

In the **Selection Panel**, click **Add Type**. Add a **Unity/GameObject** resource and drag your Prefab into the prototype list.

To switch which object gets placed, simply select a different prototype in the Selection Panel.

---

## Step 3 — Configure placement settings

In the **Settings Panel**, adjust:

- **Raycast** settings to control how the object aligns to the surface under the cursor.
- **Transform Components** (rotation, scale offset) for fine-tuned placement defaults.

---

## Step 4 — Click to place in the scene view

Move your cursor into the **Scene View**. A preview of the object follows the cursor. Click to place it at that exact position, aligned to the surface.

---

## What's next

- Use [Edit](/megaworld/getting-started/edit) to adjust individual objects after placing them.
- Use [Pin](/megaworld/getting-started/pin) to lock specific objects in place and protect them from brush operations.
- Read the full [Precise Place](/megaworld/tools/precise-place) reference documentation.
