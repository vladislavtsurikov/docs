Precise Physics is a PhysX Painter tool for more deliberate physics-based placement.

Where **Brush Physics** is optimized for brush-style repeated strokes, **Precise Physics** is closer to targeted placement with spacing and an explicit placement point.

<Info>
  New to Precise Physics? Follow the step-by-step [Precise Physics — Getting Started](/megaworld/getting-started/precise-physics) tutorial first.
</Info>

## Supported resource types

Precise Physics supports:

- [Terrain Object](/megaworld/resource-types/terrain-object)
- [Unity/GameObject](/megaworld/resource-types/unity-gameobject)

## Tool-level controls

Precise Physics uses:

- **Precise Physics Tool**
- **Physics Effects**

The confirmed tool settings are:

- [Spacing](/megaworld/shared-settings/spacing) — minimum distance between consecutive placements.
- [Position Offset Y](/megaworld/shared-settings/position-offset) — shifts the spawn point above the surface before physics simulation begins.

## Shared systems used by this tool

Precise Physics uses:

- [Physics Transform Settings](/megaworld/shared-settings/physics-transform-settings)
- [Success](/megaworld/shared-settings/success)
- **Physics Effects**

## When to use Precise Physics

Precise Physics is best when you want:

- more intentional physics placement than a broad brush stroke;
- to place objects one action at a time with a clear preview point;
- physics-based settling without switching to a large-area stamper workflow.

## Notes

- Precise Physics is part of the **PhysX Painter** family.
- The scene view shows a direct placement marker instead of a brush volume.
- The selected prototype is chosen from the active group, then placed through the physics workflow.
