Every tool has its own settings, and many tools also expose different settings depending on the selected resource type.

## How settings are organized

MegaWorld usually combines two layers:

- **Tool settings** that control how the tool behaves overall.
- **Prototype settings** that control how each selected item behaves inside that tool.

## Shared systems you will see often

Several systems appear across multiple tools:

- [Mask Filters](/megaworld/mask-filters/overview)
- [Transform Components](/megaworld/transform-components/overview)
- [Overlap Check Settings](/megaworld/overlap-check-settings/overview)
- [Spawn Detail Settings](/megaworld/general-settings/spawn-detail-settings)
- [Terrain Detail Settings](/megaworld/general-settings/terrain-detail-settings)
- [Additional Spawn Settings](/megaworld/general-settings/additional-spawn-settings)

## Resource-type differences

The same tool can expose different settings depending on whether you selected:

- a GameObject;
- a Terrain Detail;
- a Terrain Texture;
- another supported resource type for that tool.

That is why some screenshots or examples from one workflow do not match another workflow exactly, even inside the same tool.

<Tip>
  When a tool feels complicated, break the Settings panel into layers: first tune the tool-level controls, then tune one prototype at a time, and only after that add advanced systems such as filters or transform stacks.
</Tip>
